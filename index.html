The Situation

Facts:


  • Jeff Lebowski assigned to help Nagelbett install equipment for Treehorn Inc.
  • Nagelbett's equiment arrives late...only 8/10 installations complete.
  • Walter Sobchak offers to finish remaining installations.
  • Recent accidents due to improper installation prompted Nagelbett to send engineers to supervise.
  • Potential for serious injury.

Scenario A

Facts:


  • Installations supposed to be completed on the 12th, vacation on the 13th.
  • Jeff already put down money for the cabin.
  • Chief engineer Donny Kerabatsos cannot officially approve Jeff's leaving before job is complete.
  • Jeff has no doubt Walter is capable of finishing the installations.
  • Nagelbett is not required by law nor contract to supervise.

Ethical Issues:


  • Deontology - has a duty to complete his job in order to avoid risk of injury.
  • Idealism - one should never lie.
  • Utilitarianism - give up his own vacation to potentially save the lives of many.
  • Pragmatism - if Walter is in fact capable, taking his vacation would be good for everyone.

Possible Solutions:


  • Jeff does not go on vacation.
  • Jeff goes on vacation.
  • Jeff goes on vacation, but comes back once the parts arrive.
  • Jeff goes on vacation but supervises via remote methods.
  • Postpone installation until after vacation.

Professional Recommendation:


Go on vacation, but come back once the parts arrive!

Scenario B

Facts:


  • Jeff has contractual deadline with other major customer who is insistent on getting the work done on time.
  • Jeff tells Walter he will stop by on his way back to check, though he can't check everything.
  • Walter must sign papers verifying that Jeff supervised the installations.

Ethical Issues:


  • Deontology - has a duty to complete his job in order to avoid risk of injury.
  • Idealism - one should never lie.
  • Utilitarianism - give up major client to potentially save the lives of many OR give up smaller client to avoid legal trouble.
  • Legal - has binding contract with major client, and must have Walter sign contract.
  • Pragmatism - choose obligation which would be more beneficial to the company.

Possible Solutions:


  • Get another engineer to do it.
  • Delay the first project.
  • Delay the second project.
  • Go on to second project but sign papers saying he supervised the first.
  • Do second project but do not sign papers saying he supervised.

Professional Recommendation:


Get another engineer to do it!

Problems with the Scenario:


  • How far away is his vacation -- is travel realistic?
  • What is this second project -- how long will it take?
  • Is safety at stake with the second project?
  • Does Nagelbett have sufficient resources/manpower -- is Jeff the only engineer that can do this?
  • What sort of contracts are involved?
